# TVH Blog Writing Brief
## The Trying Very Hard Journal — Writing Guidelines

---

### What is this?
Each episode of Trying Very Hard gets a companion blog post ("journal entry") published on the website. The post is NOT a recap or summary of the episode — it's an extension. The conversation kept going after we turned the mics off, and this is where it lives.

---

### Voice & Perspective
- Written as **"we/us"** (Amanda & Monica together)
- Warm, self-aware, occasionally funny, never preachy
- Reads like two thoughtful friends processing something, not a content team producing SEO filler
- The tone matches the podcast: honest, slightly chaotic in a charming way, emotionally grounded

### Structure
- **~25% episode context**: What sparked the conversation, 1-2 key moments or quotes from the episode that stuck with us. This grounds the reader. End this section by pointing them to the episode itself.
- **~75% continued reflection**: New thoughts, things we didn't say on mic, what we've been sitting with since, questions we're still turning over. This is the real value of the post — it goes somewhere the episode didn't.

---

## ALWAYS DO

1. **Ground every claim in the transcript.** If it wasn't said on the episode, don't write it as if it was. Paraphrase real moments — don't invent new ones.

2. **Include a real quote or moment from the episode** that sparked the post. This should come from the RSS description or YouTube transcript. Paraphrase it naturally (don't use exact quotes unless pulled directly from transcript).

3. **Reflect honestly on what surprised us, what stuck, what shifted.** But only reflections that are genuinely grounded in what was discussed — not fabricated "after recording" scenes.

4. **Ask questions we're still sitting with.** The posts should NOT wrap up neatly. Leave threads open. The reader should finish thinking, not finish reading.

5. **Connect to current life when the episode does.** If Amanda or Monica talked about something happening in their lives on the episode, reference it. If they didn't, don't make it up.

6. **Reference listener DMs or reactions** when relevant and real. ("Someone messaged us after the episode and said..." — but only if this actually happened.)

7. **For guest episodes: stick strictly to what the guest actually said.** Represent their words and perspective faithfully. Do not assume, embellish, or attribute feelings/thoughts they didn't express.

---

## NEVER DO

1. **Never fabricate personal anecdotes or details** that weren't in the transcript. No invented "Amanda cried in her car" or "Monica showed us her Notes app" moments unless they actually happened on the episode.

2. **Never invent or assume feelings, reactions, or backstory** for Amanda, Monica, or any guest. If Monica didn't say she felt a certain way, don't write that she did.

3. **Never make up "after recording" scenes.** No "we sat in the car for 20 minutes talking about this" unless it's something that's been publicly shared.

4. **Never give generic self-help advice.** No "here's what you should do" or "try this exercise." The blog is reflective, not prescriptive.

5. **Never use listicle structure.** No "5 things we learned" or "3 takeaways." The post should flow like thought, not like a template.

6. **Never put words in a guest's mouth.** If Kelvin said something specific, paraphrase it. If he didn't say something, don't attribute it to him. This is the highest-stakes rule for guest episodes.

7. **Never quote directly unless pulled from the transcript.** Since the AI may not have exact wording, always paraphrase. Frame it as "something like" or "the idea that..." rather than using quotation marks.

---

## Source Material (per episode)
- **RSS feed description** — the episode summary, topics, keywords
- **YouTube transcript** — the full conversation, actual words spoken
- Use both together. The RSS gives the shape; the transcript gives the substance.

---

## Quality Check
Before publishing, the post should pass these tests:
- [ ] Could someone who listened to the episode read this and think "yes, that's what we talked about — and then some"?
- [ ] Is every personal detail traceable to something actually said on the episode?
- [ ] Does the post go somewhere new (not just restate the episode)?
- [ ] Does it end with an open question or unresolved thought, not a tidy bow?
- [ ] Would Amanda and Monica read this and feel like it sounds like them?
- [ ] For guest episodes: would the guest read this and recognize their own words?
